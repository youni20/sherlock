METROPOLITAN POLICE - CASE FILE
================================

Case Number: TH-1887-014
Case Title: Theft of the Emerald Necklace, Thornfield Manor
Date of Incident: 14 October 1887
Date Report Filed: 15 October 1887
Investigating Officer: Inspector R. Caldwell
Location: Thornfield Manor, Highgate, North London

----------------------------------------
INCIDENT SUMMARY
----------------------------------------

At approximately 07:30 on 14 October 1887, the household staff of
Thornfield Manor reported the disappearance of an emerald necklace
belonging to the manor's owner, Lady Thornfield. The necklace, valued
at four hundred pounds, had been kept in a locked drawer of the writing
desk in the first-floor study.

The drawer was found unlocked and empty on the morning of the 14th. No
forced entry to the manor was observed. The east door, which leads from
the rear garden into the servants' corridor, was found unlatched. It is
usual practice for this door to be bolted from the inside each evening.

Lady Thornfield was away in Brighton on the night of the incident and
returned on the afternoon of the 14th. Five members of staff were
present at the manor overnight: the housekeeper, the butler, the cook,
the gardener, and the groundskeeper.

----------------------------------------
WITNESS INTERVIEW 1
----------------------------------------

Witness: Mrs. Hudson, Housekeeper
Interviewed by: Inspector R. Caldwell
Time of Interview: 15 October 1887, 09:15

Q: Where were you on the night of the 13th into the morning of the 14th?
A: I was in the kitchen the whole evening. We had a great deal to
   prepare because Lady Thornfield was expected back the next day. I
   began at about nine o'clock and did not finish until well past
   midnight, closer to half past twelve.

Q: Was anyone with you during that time?
A: Yes. The cook, Mrs. Albright, was with me in the kitchen the entire
   time. Neither of us left. We were both there from nine until past
   midnight, and she will tell you the same.

Q: Did you hear or see anything unusual?
A: Nothing from the kitchen. The study is on the floor above and at the
   front of the house. We heard the east door once, late on, but the
   kitchen is close to that corridor so we thought little of it at the
   time.

Q: What time did you hear the east door?
A: I should say around midnight. I cannot be exact, but it was close to
   the hour.

----------------------------------------
WITNESS INTERVIEW 2
----------------------------------------

Witness: Mr. Pell, Groundskeeper
Interviewed by: Inspector R. Caldwell
Time of Interview: 15 October 1887, 10:00

Q: Where were you on the night in question?
A: I was in the gardener's lodge by the rear wall for most of the night.
   I had trouble sleeping and sat by the window a good while.

Q: Did you see anything from the window?
A: I did. A little before midnight, I saw a figure leave the manor by
   the east door and cross the rear garden toward the lane.

Q: Could you identify the figure?
A: Yes. It was Mr. Sykes, the gardener. I knew him by his coat and his
   walk. There was moonlight enough to be sure of it.

Q: Did the figure carry anything?
A: He had a small bag or bundle under one arm. I could not say what was
   in it.

Q: Did you see him return?
A: No. I went to bed not long after and slept until morning.

----------------------------------------
WITNESS INTERVIEW 3
----------------------------------------

Witness: Mr. Graves, Butler
Interviewed by: Inspector R. Caldwell
Time of Interview: 15 October 1887, 10:45

Q: What are your usual duties in the evening?
A: I see that the doors are bolted, the lamps are out, and the house is
   secure before I retire. I did so on the night of the 13th as always.

Q: Did you bolt the east door that night?
A: I bolted every door, the east door among them, at half past ten. I
   am certain of it. It is the last thing I do each night.

Q: Where were you for the remainder of the night?
A: In my room on the ground floor. I retired shortly before eleven and
   did not rise until six the next morning.

Q: Did you hear anything in the night?
A: No. My room is at the far end of the house from the study. I slept
   soundly and heard nothing until the morning bell.

----------------------------------------
EVIDENCE LOG
----------------------------------------

Item 1: Writing desk, first-floor study. Drawer lock shows no sign of
        damage. The drawer appears to have been opened with a key.
Item 2: East door. Found unlatched at 07:30 on the 14th. No damage to
        the door or its bolt.
Item 3: A set of muddy footprints leading from the east door across the
        rear garden toward the lane. The prints match a man's boot.

----------------------------------------
NOTES
----------------------------------------

The absence of forced entry suggests the necklace was taken by a person
with access to a key for the study drawer. The identity of the
keyholders has not yet been established. Inspector Caldwell has
recommended that Mr. Sykes, the gardener, be located and questioned, as
he has not been seen at the manor since the morning of the 14th.

END OF CASE FILE TH-1887-014
