METROPOLITAN POLICE - CASE FILE
================================

Case Number: MP-1889-073
Case Title: Disappearance of Mr. Albert Finch
Date of Incident: 02 March 1889
Date Report Filed: 04 March 1889
Investigating Officer: Sergeant T. Mowbray
Location: Greenwich, South-East London

----------------------------------------
INCIDENT SUMMARY
----------------------------------------

Mr. Albert Finch, a clerk employed at the Greenwich Shipping Office, was
reported missing by his sister, Miss Eleanor Finch, on the morning of
04 March 1889. Mr. Finch was last seen leaving the shipping office at
the close of business on 02 March 1889 at approximately 18:00.

Mr. Finch did not return to his lodgings on the night of the 2nd and did
not appear at the office on the 3rd. His landlady confirmed his bed had
not been slept in. There is no indication of a struggle at his lodgings,
and his personal effects, including a travelling case, were found to be
missing from his room.

----------------------------------------
WITNESS INTERVIEW 1
----------------------------------------

Witness: Miss Eleanor Finch, sister of the missing man
Interviewed by: Sergeant T. Mowbray
Time of Interview: 04 March 1889, 11:00

Q: When did you last see your brother?
A: On Sunday last, the 1st of March. He came to tea. He seemed in good
   spirits, though he spoke of money troubles, as he often did.

Q: Did he mention any plans to travel?
A: He said nothing to me of travelling. But Albert was a private man.
   He did not always tell me his business.

Q: Do you know of anyone who wished him harm?
A: No. Albert kept to himself. He had few friends and, so far as I know,
   no enemies.

----------------------------------------
WITNESS INTERVIEW 2
----------------------------------------

Witness: Mr. Dobbs, foreman at the Greenwich Shipping Office
Interviewed by: Sergeant T. Mowbray
Time of Interview: 04 March 1889, 14:30

Q: When did you last see Mr. Finch?
A: On the evening of the 2nd. He left the office at six o'clock with the
   rest of the clerks. Nothing seemed amiss.

Q: Had Mr. Finch behaved unusually in the days before?
A: Now that you ask, he had been anxious. He twice asked the manager for
   an advance on his wages and was twice refused. On the 2nd he cleared
   his desk of his personal papers, which I thought odd at the time.

Q: Did he say where he was going that evening?
A: He did not. He bid us good evening and walked toward the river.

----------------------------------------
EVIDENCE LOG
----------------------------------------

Item 1: Mr. Finch's lodgings. Bed unslept in. Travelling case and
        several articles of clothing absent.
Item 2: A ticket stub recovered from the floor of Mr. Finch's room,
        partly torn. The legible portion reads "...HAMPTON" and a fare
        of three shillings.
Item 3: Mr. Finch's desk at the shipping office. Personal papers
        removed; office papers left in order.

----------------------------------------
NOTES
----------------------------------------

Sergeant Mowbray notes that the removal of Mr. Finch's travelling case
and personal papers, together with the recovered ticket stub, may
indicate that Mr. Finch departed London of his own accord. No evidence
of foul play has been found. Enquiries are being made at the railway
stations serving destinations ending in "hampton".

END OF CASE FILE MP-1889-073
